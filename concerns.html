Nevada’s Real Concerns

This page is not a victory lap. It’s a list of what keeps Lew up at night.

Campaigns love to celebrate “results.” This page is about something different: the problems Nevada families, students, workers, and seniors still feel every single day. These are the concerns Lew Whitten believes must be faced honestly before Nevada can move forward.

Economy & Cost of Living

Nevada’s numbers may look good on paper, but too many people still feel like they’re one bad month away from crisis. A healthy economy isn’t just about charts and rankings — it’s about whether regular Nevadans can breathe.

Concern
Paycheck vs. Reality
Wages have not kept pace with housing, food, gas, and insurance costs.

Lew’s Focus
Real Cost of Living
Policy should be judged by one question: can a working Nevadan support a family without constant panic? If not, something is wrong.

Small Business Squeeze

Lew’s Focus
Level Playing Field
Nevada’s future should not be auctioned off to the highest bidder. Entrepreneurs and small shops deserve a clear, simple path to survive and grow.
Concern
Booms Without Safety Nets
We celebrate record revenues, but many Nevadans have no cushion for medical emergencies, job loss, or rent spikes.
Hover for Lew’s focus
Lew’s Focus
Stability, Not Just Growth
A strong state doesn’t just boast numbers — it builds policies that keep families from collapsing when life happens.

Education & Students

Nevada has poured billions into education, yet the results are still far from where parents, teachers, and students need them to be. Lew sees this from both sides — as a former Special Education student and as a current Special Education teacher.

Concern
Spending vs. Outcomes
Historic funding increases haven’t translated into historic improvements in reading, writing, and math.

Lew’s Focus

Concern

Lew’s Focus
Support in the Classroom
Policies must protect instructional time, enforce basic expectations for behavior, and keep teachers from drowning in bureaucracy.

Students Left Behind

Nevada families, seniors, and young people are navigating a healthcare system that is expensive, confusing, and often slow to respond when help is needed most.

Concern

Many Nevadans feel like big decisions are made far away, behind closed doors, while ordinary people get talking points instead of straight answers.

Concern
Government Overreach
